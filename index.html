About

I am a first-year Ph.D. student in the Natural Language Processing Group at Nanjing University, fortunate to be advised by Prof. Zhen Wu and Prof. Xinyu Dai. My research interests lie broadly in Natural Language Processing and Multimodal Large Language Models, with a focus on personalized dialogue systems, explainable AI, and geometric perception for MLLMs.

Persona-Aware Alignment Framework for Personalized Dialogue Generation

Guanrong Li*, Xinyu Liu*, Zhen Wu, Xinyu Dai

Transactions of the Association for Computational Linguistics, Vol. 13, pp. 1722–1742, 2025

We propose PAL, a Persona-Aware Alignment Framework that directly treats persona alignment as the training objective, employing a two-stage method with Persona-Aware Learning and Persona Alignment to improve persona sensitivity in dialogue generation.

Counterfactual Language Reasoning for Explainable Recommendation Systems

Guanrong Li, Haolin Yang, Xinyu Liu, Zhen Wu, Xinyu Dai

arXiv preprint arXiv:2503.08051, 2025

We introduce CausalX, a framework integrating structural causal models with LLMs to ensure causal consistency in recommendation pipelines, with a debiasing mechanism to disentangle genuine user preferences from conformity bias.

GePBench: Evaluating Fundamental Geometric Perception for Multimodal Large Language Models

Shangyu Xing, Changhao Xiang, Yuteng Han, Yifan Yue, Zhen Wu, Xinyu Liu, Zhangtai Wu, Fei Zhao, Xinyu Dai

arXiv preprint arXiv:2412.21036, 2024

We introduce GePBench, a novel benchmark designed to assess the geometric perception abilities of MLLMs, revealing significant deficiencies in current state-of-the-art models and demonstrating that training with GePBench data yields improvements on a wide range of tasks.
